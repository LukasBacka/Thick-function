\begin{abstract}
The Lambert W function, giving the solutions of a
simple transcendental equation has become a famous
function and arises in many applications in combinatorics,
physics, or population dynamics just to mention a few.
Lambert W function was generalised by generalised Lambert
W function \cite{31} \cite{32}. In this article we develop 
new more general function, Thick function. The main goals 
of this document are to find simplified formulas for the 
following terms

\begin{align}
        \mathcal{I}(P_m, \Omega_m, n) &\equiv \lim_{\omega
        \to 0} \frac{d^{n-1}}{d\omega^{n-1}} \omega^n 
        \left(\hat{\mathbb{D}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} 
        P_m \\ \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| \omega \right) 
        - \hat{\mathbb{D}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} P_m \\
        \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| 0 \right)\right)^{-n}
        \label{nn}
\end{align}

\begin{align}
    \left[\hat{\mathbb{D}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} P_m \\ 
    \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| x  \right)\right]^{(\xi)}, \ 
    & \     \left[\hat{\mathbb{T}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} 
    P_m \\ \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| x  \right)\right]^{(\xi)}
\end{align}

\begin{align}
    J_\xi\left[\hat{\mathbb{D}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} P_m 
    \\ \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| x  \right)\right], \ & \    
    J_\xi\left[\hat{\mathbb{T}}\left( \xi_k \begin{matrix} P_m 
    \\ \Omega_m \end{matrix} \bigg| x  \right)\right]
\end{align}

\end{abstract}
